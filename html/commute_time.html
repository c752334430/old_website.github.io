Random-Walk Computation of Similarities between Nodes of a Graph with Application to Collaborative Recommendation (2007)

Introduced Commute time.

Posted by Tianyi Chen on Mar 13, 2019

Ideas:

1. Random Walk and Markov-Chain model.

2. Euclidean commute Time Distance, and pseudoinverse of the Laplacian Matrix of the graph (elements of L+ are the inner products of the node vectors in a Euclidean space preserving the ECTD).

concepts:
1. Average first passage time, m(k|i), is the average number of steps that a random walker from i would take to get to k, where k!=i.
2. Average first passage cost, o(k|i), is the average cost ... Noticed that m(k|i) is a special case of o(k|i) when every step's cost equals to 1.
3. Average commute time, n(i,j) = m(i|j)+m(j|i). Also called as "commute time distance" and "resistance distance"

How to compute? with Moore-Penrose pseudoinverse (L+). commute time = Vol(G) * (ei-ej)T * L+ * (ei-ej)
